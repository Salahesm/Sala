Profile

I work at the intersection of educational technology, learning sciences, and human-centered design. My work includes research on learning games and assessment, as well as design and evaluation of AI-powered learning tools.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Educational Technology (2023–Present), University of Florida. Advisor: Dr. Seyedahmad Rahimi.

Core Expertise

  • Learning games & gamification research (motivation, skills, outcomes).

  • Research methods: qualitative and quantitative data collection, analysis, and reporting.

Research & Interests

My research centers on designing, studying, and evaluating technology-rich learning experiences and assessment systems, including serious games and AI-supported learning tools.

Critical Thinking & Assessment

Stealth assessment, evidence-based reasoning, complex problem solving

  • Designing assessment approaches embedded within authentic learning activities.
  • Developing systems that elicit and evaluate reasoning processes, not only final answers.

Generative AI in Education

AI-powered learning tools, creativity assessment, classroom-ready design

  • Building and evaluating AI-powered platforms and learning applications.
  • Exploring rubric-based and human-in-the-loop approaches for valid, usable AI outputs.

Game-Based Learning

Digital games, motivation, 21st-century skills, growth mindset, executive functions

  • Systematic reviews on learning games and student outcomes.
  • Design implications for game mechanics aligned with learning goals and assessment.
